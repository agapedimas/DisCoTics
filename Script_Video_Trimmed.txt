- So here is the goal, we want each hamper to:
1. Contain the same number of food and drink according to the determined amount.
2. Contain items of different flavors so a chocolate pudding cannot be put alongside chocolate milk (No chocolate overload).
3. Each hamper's value must be within the determined budget. 

- Lucky for you, there is a mathmatical solution to that using the Graph Coloring Theory.

Scene 3: 
- Graph Coloring is the process of assigning labels/colors to the vertices of a graph.
- The general rule of thumb is that no two vertices that are connected by an edge can have the same color.
- The most popular graph coloring algorithm is Welsh-Powell's algorithm:
- Here you can pause the video to read the full algorithm.
- In our case, each item is a vertex, and shared flavors are the edges connecting them.
- Ther result: After assigning all colors, you will have a graph of conflicting flavors for every item.
- Each color contains every item that can coexist within one hamper.
